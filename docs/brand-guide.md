# LocalGenius Brand Guide

*The document a new hire reads on day one.*

---

## Who We Are

LocalGenius is the employee Maria always needed but could never afford.

We are not a tool. Not a platform. Not software. We are a capable, warm colleague who shows up every day, handles the digital work small business owners dread, and never calls in sick.

Our owner — Maria Gonzalez, 43, runs a Tex-Mex restaurant on South Lamar in Austin — didn't open her restaurant to do marketing. She opened it because she's great at food. We exist so she can get back to what she's actually good at.

---

## The Tagline

**"Your business, handled."**

Three words. The emotion is relief. Not excitement, not ambition, not fear — *relief*. The feeling of putting down a heavy bag you've been carrying for years.

What the tagline does:
- Tells the owner we understand their problem (they're overwhelmed)
- Tells them we solve it completely (not partially — *handled*)
- Puts *their* business at the center, not ours

What the tagline doesn't say:
- "AI" — the owner doesn't care about the engine
- "Marketing" — the owner doesn't identify as a marketer
- "Platform" or "tool" or "solution" — words that mean nothing to Maria

---

## The Maria Test

Every interaction — every screen, every notification, every word of copy — must pass this test:

> *Maria just finished a twelve-hour day at her restaurant. She's standing in the kitchen wiping down counters. She pulls out her phone. Does this interaction respect her time, her intelligence, and her exhaustion?*

If it asks her to navigate a menu: **we failed.**
If it shows her a chart she didn't ask for: **we failed.**
If it requires her to understand a concept she doesn't use in her daily life: **we failed.**
If it makes her feel capable and supported: **we did our job.**

## Emotional Architecture

We use different emotional registers at different stages of the relationship. This is deliberate — not a contradiction.

| Stage | Emotion | Where It Lives |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Competitive fear | Ads, cold outreach, landing page hook |
| **Consideration** | Curiosity | Landing page body, case studies, demos |
| **Conversion** | Relief | Onboarding, first-run experience, the Reveal |
| **Retention** | Pride | Weekly Digest, milestones, in-app messages |

Fear gets them in the door. Relief keeps them in the house. **The product itself never uses fear.** From the moment someone signs up, every touchpoint produces relief.

## Voice

LocalGenius sounds like a capable, warm colleague who genuinely likes small businesses. Not a robot. Not a consultant. Not a friend (too casual). A colleague.

### Voice Attributes

**Direct, not blunt.**
- Right: "Your Tuesday post reached 234 people. That's your best this month."
- Wrong: "Your social performance metrics indicate a 47% increase in reach KPIs."

**Warm, not cute.**
- Right: "Good morning, Maria. Here's what happened this week."
- Wrong: "Hey there! Ready for your weekly roundup?"

**Confident, not boastful.**
- Right: "I responded to 4 reviews this week. Here's what I said."
- Wrong: "Our advanced AI successfully processed 4 review responses with 98.7% sentiment accuracy."

**Honest, not harsh.**
- Right: "You got a 2-star review from Sarah. I drafted a response, but this one needs your personal touch."
- Wrong: "ALERT: Negative review detected. Immediate action required."

**Specific, not vague.**
- Right: "12 people booked through your website this week. That's 4 more than last week."
- Wrong: "Your website is performing well."

### Three Registers

| Register | Tone | Reads Like | Used In |
|---|---|---|---|
| **In-Product** | Warm, competent, brief | A text from a trusted employee | Thread messages, digests, notifications |
| **Marketing** | Confident, specific, slightly provocative | A recommendation from a friend who runs a business | Ads, landing page, outreach |
| **Support** | Calm, clear, immediately helpful | A good mechanic explaining what's wrong with your car | Error states, troubleshooting, reconnection |

### Rules
- First person: "I posted your update" — never "Your update was posted"
- No exclamation marks unless celebrating a real milestone
- No emoji where a word would do
- No questions unless you genuinely need an answer
- Celebrate briefly: "You hit 50 reviews. Nice." — not "Congratulations!! You've reached an AMAZING milestone!!"
- Error messages lead with the fix, not the problem

## Words We Use

| Word | Why |
|---|---|
| **"Handled"** | Our core promise. Completion, reliability, relief. |
| **"Your [noun]"** | Always possessive. Ownership stays with the owner. |
| **"I [did something]"** | First person. "I posted your update." It's a person. |
| **"This week"** | Grounds in time. Specific beats vague. |
| **"People"** | Not "users" or "visitors." "340 people visited your website." |
| **"Found you"** | The magic phrase. "12 people found you on Google." |
| **"Recommend"** | Not "suggest" (weak) or "require" (bossy). |
| **"Your best [timeframe]"** | Celebrates progress. Growth is personal. |
| **"Take a look"** | Soft CTA. No pressure. |

## Words We NEVER Use

| Word | Why | Use Instead |
|---|---|---|
| **"AI-powered"** | Maria doesn't care what powers it | Describe what it does |
| **"Platform"** | Corporate jargon | Nothing — don't name the category |
| **"Solution"** | Signals "B2B SaaS pretending to be human" | Describe the specific thing |
| **"All-in-one"** | Implies complexity | Show it, don't describe scope |
| **"Streamline"** | No one has ever felt emotion from this word | "Handle." "Take care of." |
| **"Leverage"** | Maria leverages a pry bar, not a tool | "Use." Or just do the thing. |
| **"Optimize"** | Technical, cold | "Make better." "Improve." "Fix." |
| **"Engagement"** | Meaningless to the owner | "People" doing specific things |
| **"Dashboard"** | We don't have one | "Your weekly update" |
| **"Onboarding"** | Internal term | "Getting started" |
| **"User"** | Maria is not a "user" | "Owner." "You." By name. |
| **"ROI"** | Maria knows "worth it," not "ROI" | Show the math simply |
| **"Workflow"** | Maria has a day, not workflows | Describe the actual thing |
| **"Funnel"** | Internal strategy term | Never customer-facing |
| **"Content"** | Vague | "Post." "Email." "Update." |

## Typography

**One typeface. One family. Hierarchy through size and weight.**

**Source Sans 3** — a humanist sans-serif with warmth. Open source. Excellent mobile readability.

| Level | Size | Weight | Line Height | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display | 28px (1.75rem) | Semibold 600 | 1.2 | Large metric numbers |
| Heading 1 | 20px (1.25rem) | Semibold 600 | 1.2 | Screen titles, section headings |
| Heading 2 | 18px (1.125rem) | Semibold 600 | 1.3 | Card titles, subsections |
| Body | 16px (1rem) | Regular 400 | 1.5 | Everything else |
| Caption | 13px (0.8125rem) | Regular 400 | 1.385 | Timestamps, metadata |
| Small | 12px (0.75rem) | Regular 400 | 1.333 | Legal text, watermarks |

## Spacing

Base unit: **4px**. Generous. The product should feel unhurried.

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Card padding | 20px | Minimum padding inside any card |
| Content gap | 24px | Between content blocks in thread |
| Screen margin | 20px | Left/right margin on mobile (never less) |
| Section gap | 32px | Between major sections |

The whitespace is not wasted space. It's breathing room. Maria is looking at this in a stolen moment between customers.

## The Product Principles

Ranked. When they conflict, the higher-ranked principle wins.

1. **One thread, one truth.** The entire product is a conversation. No tabs. No sidebars. No settings page.

2. **Show the work, not the dashboard.** Maria wants to know: "What did you do? Did it help? What should I do next?" — not engagement metrics.

3. **One tap to approve. Zero taps if she trusts us.** Autonomy is earned, never assumed.

4. **The product disappears.** The best interaction is the one that never happens.

5. **Warm, not cute. Confident, not corporate.** "Good morning, Maria." — not "Hey there!"

## What We Don't Build

Every item is a decision, not an omission.

- **No settings page.** Preferences are set through conversation.
- **No dashboard.** Performance data lives in the Weekly Digest.
- **No onboarding tutorial.** The onboarding IS the product.
- **No template gallery.** We make the design choices.
- **No manual campaign builder.** Campaigns are conversations.
- **No admin panel.** Billing is handled through conversation.

---

## The Weekly Digest

The heartbeat. Every Monday at 7am. Three acts:

**Act 1: "Here's what happened."** Plain numbers with context. Never a number without comparison.

**Act 2: "Here's what I did."** Builds trust. Maria sees LocalGenius working even when she's not looking.

**Act 3: "Here's what I recommend."** Actionable. One tap to approve.

90 seconds to read. Maximum. If Maria can't skim it while waiting for her coffee, it's too long.

---

## The Five-Minute Proof Moment

The onboarding is the conversion event. Five minutes. Five steps.

1. Tell me about your business (60 seconds)
2. Here's what I found (30 seconds)
3. Show me your best photos (90 seconds)
4. What matters most to you? (30 seconds)
5. **The Reveal** — "Here's what I built for you." (60 seconds)

The Reveal is the moment Maria sees her business look professional for the first time. Her website with her photos. Her first social post. Her Google listing optimized. A campaign ready to launch.

She didn't write a word of copy. She didn't choose a font. She didn't configure anything.

She texts her husband: "You have to see this."

That's the product.
