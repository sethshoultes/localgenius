# LocalGenius — Seed Deck Outline

*10 slides. Each tells one story. No slide tells two.*

---

## Slide 1: The Problem

Maria owns a Tex-Mex restaurant in Austin. She's great at food. She's terrible at marketing — and she knows it. She's tried hiring her nephew, signing up for Mailchimp, paying an agency $1,200 a month. Nothing stuck. Tonight she'll sit in her car at 10pm, open a 2-star Google review, write three response drafts, delete all of them, and drive home. There are 33 million Marias in America. Sixty percent of them have an inadequate digital presence. The tools that exist either cost too much, require too much expertise, or solve only one piece of a six-piece problem.

## Slide 2: The Solution

LocalGenius is the marketing employee Maria always needed but could never afford. One conversation replaces the six tools she's juggling. She talks to it the way she'd talk to a person: "Post something about our lunch special." "Respond to that bad review." "How did we do this month?" It handles everything — website, social media, reviews, email, SEO, and a weekly report. She approves with one tap. Over time, she doesn't even need to do that.

## Slide 3: The Product

*[Live screenshot of the conversation thread — the phone mockup from the landing page.]*

One screen. One conversation. No dashboard, no tabs, no settings page. Maria types or speaks. LocalGenius drafts, schedules, publishes, tracks, and reports back. The approval card is the core interaction: one tap to say yes, or edit if she wants her voice. The Weekly Digest arrives every Monday — 90 seconds, three acts: what happened, what I did, what I recommend.

## Slide 4: The Proof Moment

*[Screenshot of the onboarding Reveal — Step 5 with the four cascading cards.]*

Five minutes. Maria uploads three photos, confirms her business name, and taps one priority. LocalGenius builds a professional website, drafts her first social post, optimizes her Google listing, and suggests a campaign — all before she finishes her coffee. She taps "Publish everything." Confetti falls. Her business looks professional for the first time. She texts her husband: "You have to see this." That's the conversion event. Not a free trial. Not a demo. Real output in her hands before she can talk herself out of it.

## Slide 5: The Market

33 million small businesses in the US. 60% have an inadequate digital presence. The addressable market across five verticals (restaurants, salons, dental, home services, auto repair) is 2.5 million businesses. At a blended ARPU of $42/month, that's a $1.3 billion serviceable market. The PRD's $20B TAM is top-down and aspirational. Our $1.3B is bottom-up and defensible. We start with one vertical in one city: restaurants in Austin. 7,700 addressable. We need 300.

## Slide 6: Traction

Austin pilot: 300 base / 500 stretch restaurants in 90 days. One GTM person doing door-to-door outreach at restaurant counters. Five organic channels: direct outreach, Texas Restaurant Association partnerships, Chamber of Commerce, referral, and product-led growth (shareable Weekly Digest, "Posted by LocalGenius" watermark). 80%+ 30-day retention or we stop growing and fix. Hard stop at week 8. No paid ads before month 4. Retention is the only metric that matters in months 1-3.

## Slide 7: Business Model

$29/month base. $79/month pro. No contracts. LTV/CAC at base case (3% monthly churn): 9.3x. Payback period: 2.8 months. AI costs: 6.7% of revenue, well under the 15% ceiling. The real business model is the data moat: every week of usage generates business-specific intelligence — content performance, review sentiment patterns, seasonal engagement curves — that makes the product more valuable and switching more expensive. At 10,000 restaurants, we have an unparalleled real-time intelligence layer on local business marketing. Nobody else has that data.

## Slide 8: The Team

Six people. Six months. Three empathy-primary roles (CEO, Designer, GTM Lead) face the customer. Three speed-primary roles (Tech Lead, AI Engineer, Mobile Engineer) face the code. Every hire filtered for one question: "Has this person felt what our customer feels?" Total payroll: $790K-$960K/year. Sustainable for 20-24 months on a $2M seed. The CEO sells to restaurants in months 1-3 — not a sales team. The designer's first deliverable: a Figma prototype tested with three non-technical users. The GTM Lead's interview question: "You're at a restaurant counter at 2:30pm. You have 60 seconds. Go."

## Slide 9: Why We Win

The incumbents are either too expensive (Vendasta at $1,500/month, Podium at $399, Birdeye at $299) or too narrow (Wix builds a website, that's it). AI chatbots generate content but can't post, track, or report. We're different in kind: the complete loop. Understand, create, execute, measure, recommend, repeat. The owner touches it once — or never. Our second moat: vertical depth. Starting restaurants-only means our AI understands restaurant content better than any horizontal tool. And our third: the data compounds. Every week, every restaurant, every review, every post — the intelligence layer grows. Switching away means starting that intelligence over from zero.

## Slide 10: The Ask

$2M seed. 20-24 months of runway. Milestones: 300+ paying restaurants in Austin by month 3. 80%+ retention proven. Three case studies with hard ROI. Vertical expansion to salons and dental by month 6. Path to 10,000 users and $440K MRR by month 12-16. The product is built. The design system is live. The AI is real. The first restaurants are waiting. We need capital to hire the team and own Austin before anyone else figures out this category exists.
