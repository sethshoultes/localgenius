# LocalGenius — Sales Demo Script

*For walking into an Austin restaurant and showing what we do in under 4 minutes.*

---

## Before You Walk In

**Do this in the parking lot (2 minutes):**

1. Google the restaurant. Note their rating, review count, last review date.
2. Check their Instagram. Note the last post date.
3. Check their website. Does it exist? Does it look current?
4. Open LocalGenius on your phone. Have Maria's Kitchen loaded as the demo.

**You are not selling software. You are showing them an employee they didn't know they could afford.**

---

## The Approach

Wait until there's a natural break. Lunch rush is over, dinner hasn't started. 2:30-4:30pm is ideal. Ask for the owner by name if you know it. If not:

> "Hey — is the owner around? I've got something I built for restaurants in Austin and I wanted to show them. Takes about three minutes."

If the owner isn't there, **leave a card and go.** Never demo to a manager.

---

## The Demo (3.5 minutes)

### Beat 1: The Hook (30 seconds)

Start with something specific you found in the parking lot:

> "I looked you up on Google before coming in. You've got [X] reviews with a [Y] rating — that's solid. But your last review was [Z] days ago, and [competitor name] down the street has [A] more reviews than you. That gap is costing you customers."

**Never say "I have a product" or "Let me show you our platform." Just show them.**

### Beat 2: The Reveal (60 seconds)

Open Maria's Kitchen on your phone. Hand them the phone.

> "This is what one of your neighbors is using. Her name's Maria — she runs a Tex-Mex place on South Lamar."

Let them scroll. They'll see:
- The conversation thread — posts being drafted, reviews being answered
- The approval cards — "Here's what I wrote for you. Approve or edit."
- The weekly digest — numbers in plain language

**Let them hold the phone.** Don't take it back. Don't narrate every feature. Just let them read.

### Beat 3: The Proof (45 seconds)

When they look up from the phone:

> "Maria didn't write any of those posts. She didn't respond to those reviews. She didn't update her Google listing. She opened the app, tapped approve, and went back to cooking."

Pause. Then:

> "She pays twenty-nine dollars a month. Less than one table's dinner tab."

### Beat 4: The Five-Minute Test (45 seconds)

> "Tell you what — give me your business name and I'll set you up right now. Takes five minutes. If you don't like what you see, delete the app and forget about it. No credit card, no contract."

If they say yes, hand them your phone with the registration page open. **They type their own business name.** This is critical — when they see their business name on the screen during the Reveal, the emotional impact doubles.

Walk them through:
1. Business name + type → tap Continue
2. Discovery screen → "That's me"
3. Photos (use 3 of theirs from Google if they don't have their phone ready)
4. Priority → they pick what matters
5. **The Reveal** — their business name, their website, their first post

That's the moment. Watch their face. If they pull out their phone to show someone, you've won.

### Beat 5: The Close (30 seconds)

> "Your first week is free. After that, it's twenty-nine a month. I'll check in on you in a few days to see how it's going. Anything you need, just talk to LocalGenius in the app — it's a conversation, not a settings page."

Leave a card. Walk out. Don't linger.

---

## Objection Responses

**"I already do my own social media."**
> "How often do you post?" (They'll say "not as much as I should.") "That's the point. I'm not replacing what you do — I'm doing it when you can't."

**"I don't need help with marketing."**
> "How many Google reviews do you have?" (They'll know.) "How many does [competitor] have?" (They won't know.) Show them the gap on your phone. The gap is the pitch.

**"Twenty-nine bucks a month is a lot for something I don't understand."**
> "It's less than one dinner table. And it does the work of a part-time marketing person. Try it for a week free — if it doesn't save you time, cancel."

**"Can I think about it?"**
> "Of course. I set it up in five minutes — when you're ready, just go to localgenius.company and type your business name. It'll be waiting."

**"I already use [Yelp ads / Square Marketing / Mailchimp]."**
> "Good — this doesn't replace those. It handles the stuff those tools don't: writing your posts, answering your reviews, keeping your Google listing fresh. Think of it as the person who runs all those tools for you."

---

## What NOT to Say

- "AI-powered" — Maria doesn't care what powers it
- "Platform" or "solution" — nobody running a restaurant uses these words
- "ROI" — say "worth it" or "pays for itself"
- "Onboarding" — say "getting set up"
- "Let me show you a demo" — just show them. Don't announce it.
- "Our product" — say "this" while pointing at the phone
- "Users" or "customers of ours" — say "restaurants like yours" or "your neighbors"

---

## Leave-Behind

Business card with:
- **Front**: LocalGenius logo + "Your business, handled."
- **Back**: localgenius.company + "Five minutes. No credit card."

No QR code. No feature list. No tech jargon. Just the URL and the promise.

---

## Metrics That Matter

Track after every walkthrough:
1. Did they hold the phone? (Y/N)
2. Did they sign up on the spot? (Y/N)
3. If not, did they visit within 48 hours? (Y/N)
4. What objection came up first?

The phone-holding rate predicts conversion better than anything else.
