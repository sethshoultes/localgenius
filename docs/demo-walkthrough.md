# LocalGenius Demo Walkthrough

*3-minute live demo script for investors, restaurant associations, and partners.*
*Run with `NEXT_PUBLIC_DEMO_MODE=true` — no database or API keys needed.*

---

## Before You Start

- Open the app on your phone or an iPad. A laptop works but the phone sells the story — Maria lives on her phone.
- Set `NEXT_PUBLIC_DEMO_MODE=true` in your environment. This loads realistic Maria's Kitchen data with streaming AI responses.
- Rehearse the demo twice. Know the timing. The reveal at Step 5 should land at the 90-second mark — the midpoint.
- Have the Weekly Digest tab ready (it pre-loads in demo mode).

**Total runtime**: 3 minutes. Not 4. Not 5. Three.

---

## The Script

### Step 1: The Landing Page (0:00 – 0:15)

**Open**: `/landing`

**What they see**: "Your business, handled." in large terracotta type. A phone mockup showing a professional restaurant website. Three value props below.

**What you say**:

> "This is LocalGenius. Three words: your business, handled. We're building the marketing employee that every local restaurant owner needs but can't afford. An agency charges $1,500 a month. We charge $29. Let me show you what that looks like."

**Tap**: "Get started — it takes 5 minutes"

*Timing: 15 seconds. Don't linger. The landing page is the setup, not the show.*

---

### Step 2: Onboarding — Business Info (0:15 – 0:35)

**What they see**: Step 1 of onboarding. "Let's get your business set up."

**What you say**:

> "This is what Maria sees when she downloads the app at 10pm after closing her restaurant. We ask three things: your name, what you do, and where you are."

**Do**:
- Type: `Maria's Kitchen`
- Tap: **Restaurant** tile (it highlights in terracotta)
- Point to the auto-detected city: "Austin, TX — we grabbed that from her phone."

**Tap**: "Continue"

**What you say as the discovery animation loads**:

> "Now watch. We're searching Google, Yelp, Facebook, and Instagram for her business. She didn't connect anything. We just found her."

*Timing: 20 seconds. Type quickly. The discovery loading animation buys you a breath.*

---

### Step 3: Discovery Card (0:35 – 0:50)

**What they see**: Maria's Kitchen business card — 4.3 stars, 28 Google reviews, no website, no Instagram. Three competitors listed below.

**What you say**:

> "Maria has 28 reviews and a 4.3 average. Not bad. But no website, no Instagram, and Torchy's down the street has 847 reviews. We show her this — not to scare her, but to say: you've got a good foundation. Let me help you build on it."

**Point to**: The affirmation text. "We never open with what's missing. We open with what's working."

**Tap**: "That's me"

*Timing: 15 seconds. The competitor data is the hook for restaurant association audiences — they'll recognize the names.*

---

### Step 4: Photos (0:50 – 1:05)

**What they see**: "Show me what makes your business special." Photo upload grid.

**What you say**:

> "She uploads a few photos from her camera roll. Her best dishes, her team, her space. That's it. No writing copy, no choosing fonts, no configuring anything. She doesn't even need to describe herself — though she can."

**Do**: The demo has placeholder photos pre-loaded. If on a real device, tap the add button to show the photo picker UI.

**Tap**: "Continue" — the priority selector appears.

**Tap**: "Get found online" (or whichever feels right for the audience)

**What you say as the loading animation appears**:

> "Now watch this. This is the moment."

*Timing: 15 seconds. Move fast through photos — the audience doesn't need to watch you upload. The "now watch this" line sets up the reveal.*

---

### Step 5: The Reveal — The iPhone Moment (1:05 – 1:35)

**What they see**: The business name paints itself on screen. "Building something beautiful..." Then four cards cascade in, staggered 150ms apart:

1. **Her website** — fake browser chrome, hero photo with gradient, "Book a Table" and "View Menu" buttons, her star rating
2. **Her first social post** — her photo with warm copy: "Nothing beats a slow Tuesday with fresh guacamole..."
3. **Her Google listing** — optimized description, updated hours, response drafted for her latest review
4. **A campaign suggestion** — "Ask your 5 most recent customers for a Google review"

**What you say** (slowly — let them look):

> "Five minutes. She uploaded three photos and tapped four buttons. And now she has a professional website, a social post ready to publish, an optimized Google listing, and a campaign to get more reviews. She didn't write a word. She didn't choose a color. She didn't configure a single thing."

**Pause. Let it land.**

> "This is what makes Maria text her husband: 'You have to see this.'"

**Tap**: "Looks good — publish everything"

**What they see**: Sage green checkmark. "Published! Welcome to LocalGenius." Then a smooth transition into the main conversation thread.

*Timing: 30 seconds. This is the emotional peak. Don't rush it. The staggered card entrance does the work — let people's eyes follow each card as it appears.*

---

### Step 6: The Conversation — Lunch Special (1:35 – 2:00)

**What they see**: The conversation thread. LocalGenius's welcome message and a couple of pending approval cards.

**What you say**:

> "This is the entire product. One conversation. No menus, no dashboards, no settings page. Maria talks to it like she'd talk to an employee."

**Do**: Tap the input bar. Type: `Post something about our lunch special today`

**What they see**: The AI response streams in word by word (typewriter effect, 30ms per word). A social post card appears with a photo, copy, and scheduling options.

**What you say**:

> "She typed one sentence. LocalGenius wrote the copy, picked her best photo, and scheduled it for 11:30 — right before the lunch crowd starts searching."

*Timing: 25 seconds. The streaming typewriter effect is the "wow" moment for technical audiences. Let them watch it write.*

---

### Step 7: One-Tap Approve (2:00 – 2:10)

**Do**: Tap "Schedule for 11:30am" (or "Approve" on the review response card)

**What they see**: The checkmark animation — a brief, satisfying spring overshoot. The card collapses to "Scheduled ✓"

**What you say**:

> "One tap. That's all Maria does. And over time, as she approves more, LocalGenius learns to handle the easy ones automatically. She just reads the weekly report."

*Timing: 10 seconds. The checkmark animation is small but visceral — point it out if the audience is close enough to see it.*

---

### Step 8: The Weekly Digest (2:10 – 2:35)

**Do**: Tap the **Digest** icon in the bottom navigation (second of two icons).

**What they see**: "Good morning, Maria." Three metric cards: 340 website visits (up 12%), 4 new reviews (all 4+ stars), 23 bookings. A sparkline showing growth. Actions listed. A recommendation with "Send It" and "Skip" buttons.

**What you say**:

> "Every Monday at 7am, Maria gets this. Ninety seconds to read. Three parts: here's what happened, here's what I did, here's what I recommend. No dashboards. No analytics. Just a clear picture from someone she trusts."

**Point to**: The metric cards. "340 visits, up 12% — she knows it's working. 23 bookings — she knows it's paying for itself."

**Point to**: The recommendation. "And she can approve the next action right here. One tap."

*Timing: 25 seconds. The digest is where retention lives. For investors, emphasize: "This is why churn is under 3%. She sees the value every Monday."*

---

### Step 9: Negative Review Response (2:35 – 2:50)

**Do**: Tap back to **Thread**. Scroll to find the review response card (demo mode includes one).

**What they see**: A review card with a 2-star review: "Food was okay but we waited 40 minutes." An AI-drafted response below it: "Sarah, I'm sorry about the wait last Tuesday..."

**What you say**:

> "This is the moment that used to ruin Maria's night. A bad review at 10pm. She'd write three drafts and delete all of them. Now LocalGenius drafts a response — professional, empathetic, sounds like her — and she approves it with one tap. Negative reviews always get flagged for her. We never respond to a bad review without her say-so."

*Timing: 15 seconds. This resonates with every restaurant owner in the room. They've all been Maria at 10pm.*

---

### Step 10: The Close (2:50 – 3:00)

**What you say**:

> "Five minutes to set up. Three minutes a day to run. $29 a month. Three hundred Austin restaurants are already using it, and 80% of them are still here after 30 days — in a market where the average SaaS churn is 5-10% per month."

> "We're not a content generator. We're not another dashboard. We're the employee Maria always needed but could never afford. Your business, handled."

*Timing: 10 seconds. End on the tagline. Always.*

## Audience-Specific Notes

### For Investors
Emphasize at Step 8: "This is the retention engine. The Weekly Digest is why our 30-day retention is 80%+. Every Monday she sees the value. LTV/CAC is 9.3x at base case." At Step 10, add: "We're starting with restaurants in Austin. The addressable market is 7,700 in Austin alone, 693,000 nationally. At $29-79/month, this is a billion-dollar category."

### For Restaurant Association Events
Skip the metrics. Emphasize the feeling. At Step 5 (The Reveal), slow down even more — let restaurant owners in the audience see themselves in Maria. At Step 9 (negative review), ask: "Raise your hand if you've ever lost sleep over a Google review." Every hand goes up. That's your close.

### For Franchise Operators
After the standard demo, add: "Now imagine this for seven locations. Same conversational interface, but each location gets its own voice because the Supercuts in East Austin serves a different neighborhood than the one in Lakeway. The data model supports multi-location from day one. We're not building that UI yet — but when you're ready, the foundation is there."

## If Something Goes Wrong

| Problem | Recovery |
|---|---|
| Demo mode not loading | Check `NEXT_PUBLIC_DEMO_MODE=true` in env. Restart dev server. |
| Streaming doesn't animate | Demo mode simulates streaming at 30ms/word. If instant, the browser may be throttling timers — switch to Chrome. |
| Audience asks "Is this real data?" | "This is a demo with realistic data. The real product generates this from actual Google, Yelp, and Instagram data. Want to see yours? Give me your business name." |
| Someone asks about AI | "We use AI to generate the content and responses. But we never say 'AI-powered' to the owner — she doesn't care what powers it. She cares that it works." |
| Timer runs past 3 minutes | Cut Step 4 (photos) short. The reveal at Step 5 is more important than watching an upload. |

---

*Rehearse twice. Time yourself. The demo is the product — make it feel like someone is in their corner.*
